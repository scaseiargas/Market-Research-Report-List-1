<p><p>Non-thermal pasteurization methods, including High Pressure Processing (HPP), irradiation, and ultrasonic processing, enhance food safety while preserving quality. HPP utilizes high pressure to eliminate pathogens without heat, maintaining taste and nutrients. Irradiation involves exposing food to ionizing radiation, effectively reducing microbial load and extending shelf life. Ultrasonic processing employs high-frequency sound waves to disrupt cell structures, ensuring microbial inactivation. Together, these methods cater to the growing demand for minimally processed foods while ensuring safety and extending product longevity.</p></p>
