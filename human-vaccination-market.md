<p><p>The human vaccination market comprises several types of vaccines. Inactivated vaccines contain killed pathogens, providing immunity without causing disease. Live-attenuated vaccines use weakened forms of pathogens to stimulate a strong immune response. mRNA vaccines introduce messenger RNA to instruct cells to produce pathogen proteins, prompting immunity. Conjugate vaccines combine polysaccharides from pathogens with proteins to enhance immune recognition. Other market categories may include peptide, protein subunit, and viral vector vaccines, addressing diverse infectious diseases and improving public health outcomes.</p></p>
