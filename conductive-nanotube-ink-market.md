<p><p>The conductive nanotube ink market consists of two main types: high temperature resistant and ordinary type. High temperature resistant inks are designed to maintain their conductive properties and structural integrity under extreme heat, making them suitable for applications in aerospace, automotive, and electronics that require durability in harsh environments. Conversely, ordinary type inks are more versatile for standard applications, focusing on cost-effectiveness and ease of use in consumer electronics and printed circuits, where extreme temperature resistance is less critical.</p></p>
