<p><p>The power tool batteries market is primarily divided into two types: Nickel batteries and Lithium-Ion (Li-Ion) batteries. Nickel batteries, such as NiCad and NiMH, are known for their durability and cost-effectiveness, but they experience memory effects and have lower energy density. In contrast, Li-Ion batteries offer higher energy density, longer run times, and lighter weight, making them increasingly popular in modern power tools. The shift towards Li-Ion technology is driven by advancements in battery efficiency and environmental considerations.</p></p>
