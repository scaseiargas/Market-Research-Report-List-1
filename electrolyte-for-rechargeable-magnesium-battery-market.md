<p><p>The rechargeable magnesium battery market features various electrolytes, primarily categorized into triglyme-based and THF-containing electrolytes. Triglyme-based electrolytes utilize triethylene glycol dimethyl ether, known for its high ionic conductivity and stability, enhancing battery performance. Conversely, THF-containing electrolytes, using tetrahydrofuran, offer efficient ionic transport but may face stability challenges. Both electrolyte types are crucial in optimizing the efficiency, safety, and longevity of magnesium batteries, driving advancements in energy storage technology.</p></p>
