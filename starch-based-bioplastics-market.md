<p><p>The starch-based bioplastics market encompasses various processing techniques, each catering to specific applications. Blow molding is used for creating hollow objects, such as bottles, by inflating heated plastic. Extrusion involves forcing the material through a shaped die to produce continuous profiles, suitable for films and sheets. Injection molding allows for the production of complex shapes by injecting molten bioplastic into molds. Other methods like thermoforming and foaming add versatility, enabling the creation of various packaging and insulation materials.</p></p>
